Russian Space Pencils

I like problems. I like it when they’re hiding. I especially like it when they hide in plain sight. I like exploring a route from A to B via Q and G. Especially when I end up using A to B. At the height of the space race in the 1960s, NASA invested millions of dollars solving the problem of letting astronauts write in zero g. Russian designers took a simpler approach. They used a pencil. This is why I do design.

Lots of stuff here about how amazing I am at reading a brief and asking some questions. Then, more stuff detailing all the gold stars I’ve won at drawing pictures of screens. A bit about the joy of wireframes. Perhaps ending on why prototyping is da bomb.
